Hey there! It's cool that you want to learn about CO2! CO2 is a gas that comes from breathing and burning
things like coal and oil. A little bit of CO2 is okay, but too much can be bad for people and the Earth.
By checking the CO2 levels in your classroom and other places in school, you can see if the air is good or
bad. If there's too much CO2, it can make it hard to breathe and pay attention in class. You can use this
information to make changes to improve the air quality, like opening windows or using air filters.







Hey there! The data you collect is important because it tells us about the world around us. Just like how
CO2 levels can show us air quality, data can tell us about things like the weather, animals, or what people
like to eat. When you put your data in a table, it's easy to compare and understand. You can see how things
change over time, and compare your data to others'. Tables can also help you see patterns, like if CO2 levels
are always high on certain days or times.
